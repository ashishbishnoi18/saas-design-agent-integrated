PURPOSE: Convert visitors into paying customers for discounted official Carfax
reports — driving (a) one-time report purchases from individual used-car buyers
and (b) recurring dealer subscriptions for continuous report access. The page
must establish credibility as a legitimate reseller before either action feels
safe. Business intent: revenue from both channels, with dealer LTV being
substantially higher than individual LTV.

AUDIENCE: Two distinct segments that should self-select.

  Segment A — Individual buyer (one-time):
    Who: A person evaluating a specific used car they're considering buying.
    Motivation: They've found a car they like (Craigslist / Facebook
      Marketplace / dealership / private seller) and need a Carfax to vet it
      before committing.
    Knowledge: Knows carfax.com exists and costs ~$40 for one report. Doesn't
      know if discounted resellers are legitimate.
    Decision behavior: Price-sensitive — the discount is the hook. But will
      bounce hard if the page reads as scammy. Decides in under 60 seconds
      whether this is real. Trust signals (volume of users served, refund
      guarantee, BBB / payment processor logos, sample report) close the gap.

  Segment B — Dealer (continuous):
    Who: Used-car dealer or independent buyer who needs reports as a regular
      cost of doing business.
    Motivation: Already paying for Carfax (or AutoCheck). Looking for cost
      reduction at scale — even a small per-report saving multiplies across
      dozens or hundreds of monthly reports.
    Knowledge: Expert. Knows official pricing, knows the difference between
      Carfax and AutoCheck, knows what's in a report. Skeptical of anything
      that smells like a knockoff.
    Decision behavior: Methodical. Wants to see volume pricing, terms,
      legitimacy proof (e.g., authorized reseller status, business
      registration), and ideally talk to a person before committing.

CONTEXT: Standalone landing page (single page, root of the site).
  Entry: word-of-mouth referrals (warm, partial trust pre-seeded) and social
    media (cold, full skepticism).
  Exit: Either checkout flow for individuals (one report purchase) or
    contact/signup flow for dealers (volume plan inquiry).
  User journey stage: SELLING. We are pre-purchase for both segments. Trust
    establishment is the primary blocker to conversion.

KEY ACTIONS:
  1. PRIMARY — Buy a report now (single-click flow for individuals,
     enter VIN → pay → receive PDF).
  2. SECONDARY — Set up dealer access (form or contact path for volume).
  3. TERTIARY — See a sample report (low-commitment proof CTA for the
     skeptical-but-curious — works for both segments).
